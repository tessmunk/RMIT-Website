Interest in IT

Interest in IT

What is your interest in IT? When did your interest in IT start?

My interest in IT started with my Graphic Design diploma – I wanted to make websites. That slowly became something I was interested in whilst I studied but nothing really came of it. Then, with all my experience with people with disabilities - I realized how IT can open one’s world up. I saw the differences that IT was making for my clients (and even myself) during the hardest, loneliest times. These were the two main factors that sparked my interest in IT.

With designing websites, I was in the firm belief that they worked like photoshop when I first started my graphic design career. Big mistake I know. I thought you created elements by sizing them up on screen and choosing colours from a colour wheel. When I found out they were coded, I was shocked. But also intrigued. I always had thought coding was for computer scientists. It was never in reach for me. However, about a year ago, I signed up to code academy, when I had my first foot surgery. It put me out of action for physical things, but I sat on my little MacBook and went through the HTML and CSS portion of the course. I loved it! It was so strange for me, as it was completely different from photoshop, but it was rewarding. Now, I have a big PC and even bigger dreams for being able to code. I think it’s rather exciting!

Why did you choose to come to RMIT?

Earlier this year I started an Associates degree in Cyber security with UTAS. I did a beginners course in Programming, where we focused on Python. Although I really enjoyed it (even if it was hard) I felt like IT was something I could really sink my teeth into, so I took a risk an applied to study at RMIT. The degree can be studied from home which suits me perfectly, it’s got a wide variety of course material, and I can finally learn how to make websites. I also can incorporate more IT into my work life and help those I look after. I also have heard great things about RMIT from locals and friends, so I felt like I could trust the Uni to teach me well.

Home learning is a big factor for me. Due to my mental health (and the fact that I just had a second surgery on my foot, meaning I can’t travel) I need to be able to study from home. I’ve created a safe, easy going environment, which for me means mental health peace. There were a few Universities to choose from, to study IT. I choose RMIT, purely because I have a few high school friends, who went to Melbourne to study there. I saw them progress through there degrees on Facebook, and it seemed comprehensive and fun.

What do you expect to learn during your studies?

I expect to learn a whole new way of interacting with technology around me. I believe there is much that I don’t know about IT which I will be fascinated to find out about. I hope to learn much about gaming, website development, and cyber security. I also expect to learn skills including teamwork, communication and problem solving.

I am not one of those people who are not naturally in tune with computers or technology. I respect it, but I don’t vibe with it. I do however, want to learn, and I have A LOT of patience. I’m hoping that is enough to get me through this course. I feel like if I keep studying this course, I will pick up some real skills with technology surrounding cyber security, networking and more soft skills like problem-solving, critical thinking and resourcefulness.
